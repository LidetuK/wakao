Zanzibar Travel Things to Do in Zanzibar: An Adventure Lover’s Guide

Zanzibar isn’t just about beautiful beaches—it’s packed with exciting activities for every type of traveler. From water sports to cultural experiences, here are the best things to do in Zanzibar:

  1. Snorkeling & Diving
    Explore Zanzibar’s vibrant marine life at Mnemba Atoll, Leven Bank, or Chumbe Island Coral Park. With crystal-clear waters and diverse coral reefs, it’s a paradise for underwater enthusiasts.
  2. Sunset Dhow Cruise
    Hop on a traditional wooden dhow and sail along the coast as the sun sets over the Indian Ocean. Many tours include snorkeling stops and a seafood BBQ on the beach.
  3. Spice Tour
    Discover why Zanzibar is called the “Spice Island” with a guided tour of a spice farm. Learn about cloves, vanilla, cardamom, and more while tasting fresh tropical fruits.
  4. Swim with Dolphins
    Take a boat trip from Kizimkazi and swim with wild dolphins in their natural habitat. Early morning tours offer the best chance to see them up close.
  5. Explore Stone Town’s History
    Wander through Stone Town’s labyrinthine streets, visit the Old Slave Market, the Sultan’s Palace, and the Arab Fort. Don’t forget to bargain at the local markets for souvenirs.
  6. Kitesurfing in Paje
    With steady winds and shallow waters, Paje Beach is one of the best kitesurfing spots in the world. Beginners can take lessons, while pros can enjoy the ideal conditions.
  7. Visit Prison Island
    Meet the giant tortoises and snorkel in the clear waters around Changuu Island. It’s a great half-day trip from Stone Town.
  8. Jozani Forest Walk
    Spot the endangered red colobus monkeys in Jozani Forest, Zanzibar’s only national park. The mangrove boardwalk is also a serene nature experience.
  9. Try Zanzibari Cuisine
    Indulge in local dishes like urojo (Zanzibar mix), pweza wa nazi (octopus in coconut curry), and pili pili sauce. For a unique experience, dine at The Rock Restaurant.
  10. Relax on the Best Beaches
    From Nungwi to Kendwa, Paje, and Matemwe, Zanzibar’s beaches are perfect for sunbathing, swimming, or simply unwinding with a coconut in hand.
  11. Go on a Safari Blue Tour
    This full-day boat excursion includes snorkeling, a seafood lunch, and sailing on a traditional dhow. It’s one of the most popular activities in Zanzibar.
  12. Visit a Local Village
    Experience authentic Zanzibari culture by visiting a fishing village like Nungwi or Matemwe. Learn about daily life, traditional boat-building, and local crafts.
  13. Full Moon Party at Kendwa Rocks
    If you’re visiting during a full moon, don’t miss the legendary Full Moon Party at Kendwa Rocks, featuring live music, bonfires, and dancing under the stars.
  14. Stand-Up Paddleboarding (SUP)
    Glide over the calm waters of Paje or Nungwi on a paddleboard for a fun and relaxing way to enjoy the ocean.
  15. Deep-Sea Fishing
    For adventure seekers, Zanzibar offers excellent deep-sea fishing opportunities. Catch marlin, tuna, or sailfish on a chartered fishing trip.

Whether you’re an adrenaline junkie, a culture lover, or just looking to unwind, Zanzibar offers endless possibilities. Pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable island adventure!

Zanzibar Travel Places to Visit in Zanzibar: A Tropical Paradise

Zanzibar, an archipelago off the coast of Tanzania, is a dream destination with its white sandy beaches, turquoise waters, and rich cultural heritage. Whether you're seeking relaxation, adventure, or history, Zanzibar has something for everyone. Here are some must-visit places:

  1. Stone Town
    A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Stone Town is the cultural heart of Zanzibar. Wander through its narrow alleys, admire the Arab and Persian architecture, and visit landmarks like the House of Wonders, Freddie Mercury’s House, and the Old Fort. Don’t miss the vibrant Forodhani Gardens Night Market for delicious street food.
  2. Nungwi & Kendwa Beaches
    Located in the north, Nungwi and Kendwa boast some of the island’s most stunning beaches. Perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and watching breathtaking sunsets.
  3. Prison Island (Changuu Island)
    Just a short boat ride from Stone Town, Prison Island is famous for its giant Aldabra tortoises. Enjoy snorkeling in the surrounding coral reefs or relax on serene beaches.
  4. Jozani Forest
    Home to the rare red colobus monkeys, Jozani Forest is Zanzibar’s only national park. Boardwalks through the forest and mangroves offer great wildlife viewing.
  5. Spice Farms
    Zanzibar is the “Spice Island.” Visit a spice farm to see cloves, vanilla, cinnamon, and nutmeg, and taste fresh tropical fruits.
  6. The Rock Restaurant
    Iconic oceanfront dining perched on a small outcrop near Michamvi Pingwe Beach. Enjoy fresh seafood with stunning views.
  7. Mnemba Atoll
    A top snorkeling and diving spot with colorful reefs, dolphins, and tropical fish in a protected marine area.
  8. Paje Beach
    A hotspot for kitesurfing and water sports with shallow turquoise waters and a lively beach scene.
  9. Kizimkazi Village
    Famous for dolphin tours where you can swim with wild dolphins, plus a 12th-century Persian mosque.
  10. Matemwe Beach
    Secluded stretches of sand and traditional fishing culture; a great base for trips to Mnemba Island.

Zanzibar is a paradise waiting to be explored. Whether you're diving into its rich history, relaxing on pristine beaches, or indulging in spice-infused cuisine, this island will leave you enchanted.
